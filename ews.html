About the Document

EWS certificate refers to an income and asset certificate that is issued to citizens in India who fall under the economically weaker section of the society. This certificate allows citizens 10% EWS reservation in education and government jobs. If someone falls under EWS category, they shall not be eligible for any other caste reservation such as SC, ST, OBC.

Eligibility

  • Category: Must belong to the General (Unreserved) Category, not SC, ST, or OBC.
  • Family Income: Gross annual family income from all sources (salary, business, agriculture, etc.) must be below ₹8 Lakhs for the financial year preceding the application.
  • Agricultural Land: Family should not own agricultural land exceeding 5 acres.
  • Residential Property:
    • A residential flat/house of 1000 sq. ft. or more is ineligible.
    • A residential plot in notified municipalities must be below 100 sq. yards.
    • A residential plot in non-notified municipalities must be below 200 sq. yards.

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar card
  • PAN card
  • Passport-size photo
  • Proof of identification
  • Income certificate
  • Caste certificate
  • Bank statement
  • Proof of property or land
  • Domicile certificate or residential proof
  • Self-declaration or affidavit

How to Apply – Step by Step

Citizens can apply for EWS Certificate on the official website of their state government. Every state has their own official website, depending on your state website, the steps to apply for an EWS certificate may differ. Below are fundamental steps one needs to follow to apply for an EWS Certificate.


Online process:

  1. Step 1: Visit the official website of your state and login to your account.
  2. Submit Application – Visit your local registrar office or apply online through the state portal
  3. Step 2: Look for application forms and select ‘Issuance of Income and Assets Certificate for Economically Weaker Section(EWS)’ form.
  4. Step 3: Fill the application form with your information as asked and attach all the required documents.
  5. Step 4: Make a payment for the application fee and submit your application.

Offline process:

  1. Step 1: Visit the office of the revenue department or the issuing authority in your state.
  2. Step 2: Request for the application form and fill it with your information.
  3. Step 3: Attach a copy of all the asked documents.
  4. Step 4: Make a payment for the application fee and submit your application.

Typical fee Breakdown:

  • Application Fee: ₹10 - ₹50 (can be higher on some portals).
  • Court Stamp Fee: Around ₹2 (for self-declaration/affidavit). Total: Often under ₹100 in total.
  • State Portals:Aaple Sarkar for Maharashtra, services.india.gov.in for central services.
  • Designated Offices:Tehsildar office or Revenue Department.
