About the document

The Indian Voter Card (officially the Elector's Photo Identity Card (EPIC)) is an identity document issued by the Election Commission of India to Indian citizens who have reached the age of 18. It primarily serves as an identity proof for casting their ballot in the country's municipal, state, and national elections.

Eligibility

  1. Must be an Indian citizen
  2. Must be 18 years or older
  3. Must not be disqualified under any law
  4. Must be a resident of the constituency

Required Documents

  1. Addhar Card
  2. registered Mobile Number with Aadhar Card
  3. Proof of Address
  4. Birth Certificate
  5. Light Bill

Application Process

  1. Register/Login: Visit the Voters' Service Portal. If you don't have an account, click Sign-Up and register using your mobile number and email.
  2. Fill Application Form: After logging in, select 'Apply Online for Registration of New Voter' and fill out Form 6 with accurate details.
  3. Fill Personal Details: Select your State, District, and Assembly Constituency. Enter your name, date of birth, and relative's details (Father/Mother/Spouse).
  4. Upload Documents: Upload scanned copies of your proof of age, address, and a recent passport-sized photograph.
  5. Submit Application: Review all details and submit your application. You will receive an application reference number for tracking.
  6. Verification: A Booth Level Officer (BLO) may visit your residence for verification. Ensure you are available and provide any additional information if requested.
  7. Track Application: Use your reference number on the Voter's Service Portal to track the status of your application.
  8. Receive Voter Card: Once approved, your Voter Card will be sent to your registered address via post.

Where to Apply:

  1. Online: Voter's Service Portal
  2. Offline: Visit your nearest Electoral Office

Purpose of the Certificate:

  1. Identity Proof: Serves as an official identity document for Indian citizens.
  2. Voting Rights: Enables citizens to exercise their right to vote in elections.
  3. Access to Government Services: Used as a valid ID for various government services and schemes.
  4. Address Proof: Acts as proof of residence for various legal and administrative purposes.
