About the Document

An Aadhaar card is a 12-digit unique identification number issued by India's UIDAI (Unique Identification Authority of India) to residents, serving as a universal proof of identity and address, linking biometric and demographic data for accessing government services, subsidies, and financial inclusion, ensuring unique identification across the country.

Eligibility

  • Indian Citizens: All Indian citizens residing in India.
  • Newborns: Even new babies can get Aadhaar.
  • Non-Resident Indians (NRIs): With a valid Indian passport and stay of 182+ days in India.
  • Foreign Nationals: With valid Indian visas, OCI cards, or LTV, staying 182+ days in India.
  • Citizens of Nepal/Bhutan: Can apply with specific documentation.
  • NOTE: Must have resided in India for 182 days or more in the 12 months before applying

Required Documents

-For Indian Residents

  • Proof of Identity
  • Proof of Address
  • Proof of Date Of Birth
  • Proof of Relationship

-For Children

  • Proof of Identity
  • Proof of Residence
  • Birth Certificate
  • Aadhaar of any one Parent

-For OCI Cardholders

  • Valid OCI card along with valid foreign passport for residents who have stayed in India at least 182 days in the last one year is acceptable as ID proof.

For (Long Term VISA)LTV Document Holders

  • Identity and address proof in the form of a valid Long Term VISA (LTV) document and valid/expired foreign passport of origin country issued to minorities communities of Afghanistan, Pakistan and Bangladesh such as Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, Sikhs, Parsis and Christians.

-For Nepal/Bhutan Nationals

  • Passport of Nepal/ Bhutan for Nepal/ Bhutan nationals
  • In case passport is not available, any 2 of the following documents having the same address can be submitted:
    1. Nepalese/ Bhutanese Citizenship Certificate.
    2. Voter Identity Card issued by the Election Commission of Bhutan/Nepal.
    3. Limited validity Photo Identity Certificate issued by Nepalese Mission/Royal Bhutanese Mission in India.

-Other Resident Foreigners

  • Valid VISA and valid foreign passport of resident foreigners who have stayed in India for at least 182 days in the past one year is acceptable as an identity proof.

    • OR

  • Valid Registration Certificate/ Residential permit issued by FRRO/FRO to the resident foreigners (except OCI card holders, LTV document holders and Nepal/ Bhutan nationals) is acceptable as an address proof.

Application Process

  1. Download the Form: Go to the UIDAI website (uidai.gov.in) and find the "Enrolment and Update Forms" under the "Downloads" section to get Form 1.
  2. Fill the Form:
    • Tick "Enrolment" as the purpose.
    • Fill in your Name, Gender, Date of Birth/Age, and Address (C/o, House No., Street, Landmark, etc.).
    • Provide details of your Father/Mother/Guardian for children below 5 years.
    • Use only block (capital) letters.
  3. Gather Documents: Prepare original Proof of Identity (POI) and Proof of Address (PoA) documents (e.g., Passport, Birth Certificate, Ration Card, Voter ID).
  4. Visit an Enrolment Centre: Take the filled form and original documents to an Aadhaar Seva Kendra.
  5. Biometrics: Your photo, fingerprints, and iris scan will be captured.
  6. Get Acknowledgement: You'll receive an Acknowledgement Slip with an Enrolment ID (EID) to track your application.

Key Points

  • For Updates:If updating details, provide your existing Aadhaar number and fill only the fields you want to change.
  • Document-Based: For most, document-based verification is used; Introducer or Head of Family (HoF) based methods are for specific cases without documents.
  • Children: For children under 5, parents' details are mandatory; for ages 5-18, Head of Family (HoF) details and biometric of one parent are required.
Fees:
  • Demographic Update (Name, Address, DoB, etc.): ₹75 (if done separately).
  • Biometric Update (Fingerprints, Iris, Photo): ₹125 (for ages outside MBU). From Oct 2028, it's ₹150.
  • Document Update (at Centre): ₹75 (for PoI/PoA).
  • Aadhaar Printout (PVC card/e-KYC): ₹40 (will increase to ₹50 from Oct 2028).
Processing Time: 30-90 working days

Where to Apply

Online: UIDAI Portal
Offline: Aadhaar Seva Kendra(ASK)/a Bank/Post Office/a BSNL/government office
