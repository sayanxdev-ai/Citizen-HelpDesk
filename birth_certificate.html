About the Document

A birth certificate is an official record issued under the Registration of Births and Deaths Act, 1969, documenting the birth of a child. It includes key details like name, date of birth, place of birth, and parents’ details.

Eligibility

  • Parents or legal guardians of newborns.
  • Adults applying for their own certificate (if missing or for correction).

Required Documents

  1. Proof of Birth / Date of Birth
    • Hospital birth certificate or nursing home report
    • For home births, a certificate from the local authority or village head.
  2. Parent's Identity Proof
    • Aadhaar card, PAN card, passport, voter ID, or driving licence.
  3. Address Proof of Parents or Child
    • Aadhaar, utility bills, bank passbook, or registered rent agreement.
  4. Proof of Place of Birth
    • Hospital certificate, nursing home record, or declaration from local body for home births.
  5. Application Form and Child’s Photo
    • Filled registration form + passport-size photograph of the child.

    How to Apply – Step by Step

    1. Prepare Documents – Collect birth proof, parent ID, address proof, application form, and photograph.
    2. Submit Application – Visit your local registrar office or apply online through the state portal
    3. Pay Fees – Pay the prescribed registration fee.
    4. Verification – Registrar checks documents and details.
    5. Receive Birth Certificate – Get the certificate via post, in person, or download online if available.

    Within 21 Days:

    Free or a nominal fee ₹20-₹30(Normal)

    22-30 Days late:

    A small delay fee (e.g.,₹2 to ₹11).

    After 1 Year: Even higher fee (e.g., ₹10 to ₹55) and often requires a court order or magistrate approval. Extracts: Basic extracts (e.g., for a single birth) might be around ₹50 plus search fees.
