About the Document

A Permanent Account Number (PAN) is a mandatory identity document issued by the Income Tax Department of India. It is required for filing income tax returns, opening bank accounts, investing in financial instruments, and carrying out high-value transactions.

Eligibility

The following individuals and entities are eligible to apply for a PAN card:

  • Indian citizens aged 18 years and above
  • Minors (through guardians)
  • Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs)
  • Companies and LLPs
  • Partnership firms
  • Trusts and associations of persons
  • Foreign nationals and NRIs (using Form 49AA)

Required Documents

PAN Card Application by HUFs and Individuals (Being Citizens of India)

  1. Proof of Identity – Copy of: (Voter’s ID card, Passport, Aadhar card, Ration card having Applicant’s photo, Driving License, Photo ID card which are issued by central government, state government or any undertaking of public sector​)
    Original of: A certificate of identity in prescribed format signed by a Member of Legislative Assembly, Member of Parliament, Municipal Councillor or a Gazetted Officer
  2. Proof of Address – Copy of: (Electricity Bills, Voter ID card containing photograph, Aadhaar card, Passport, Passport of spouse, Statements of Bank Account,Domicile certificate allotted by government,Property Registration Documents)
    3. Note: Utility Bills and Bank/Credit card statements should be recent, preferably not more than three months old.
  3. Proof of Date of Birth – Copy of: (Birth certificate issued by any office that is certified for issuing Birth as well as Death Certificate, Aadhaar Card, Driving license, Passport,etc)

PAN Card Application by HUFs and Individuals (Not Being Citizens of India)

  1. Proof of Identity – Copy of: (Passport, OCI card that is allotted by Government of India, PIO card allotted by the Government of India, Other Citizenship ID Number, National ID Number, Taxpayer ID Number (any of these) –have to be properly attested either by the ‘Apostille’ or by any of the Indian Embassy/ High Commission/ Consulate functioning in the applicant’s home country)
  2. How to Apply – Step by Step

Option 1: Instant e-PAN (Aadhaar-based)

  1. Visit the official Income Tax e-filing portal.
  2. Click on the "Instant e-PAN" option under the "Quick Links" section
  3. On the next page, select "Get New e-PAN
  4. Enter your 12-digit Aadhaar number, confirm the details, and click "Continue".
  5. Validate your identity using the One-Time Password (OTP) sent to your Aadhaar-linked mobile number.
  6. Once processed, you can check the status and download your e-PAN from the same portal.

    7. Note: The e-PAN is a valid form of PAN, but you will not receive a physical card through this process.

Option 2: Online Application via Protean (NSDL) or UTIITSL (suitable for individuals, HUFs, companies, etc., including foreign citizens. )

  1. Visit the official Protean PAN website or the UTIITSL website.
  2. Select the appropriate application form:
    1. Form 49A for Indian citizens.
    2. Form 49AA for foreign citizens.
  3. Fill out the online form with your details, including personal information, contact details, and your Assessing Officer (AO) code.
  4. Select your preferred mode of document submission (e-KYC/e-Sign for paperless, or physically mailing documents).
  5. Pay the applicable fee online (approx. ₹107 for Indian address, higher for foreign addresses).
  6. Upon successful submission, you will receive a 15-digit acknowledgement number.
  7. Depending on the submission mode selected:
    1. Paperless (e-KYC/e-Sign): Authenticate using an Aadhaar-linked OTP. No physical documents need to be sent.
    2. Physical Documents: Print the acknowledgement form, affix photographs, sign it, and mail it along with the required identity, address, and date of birth proofs to the address provided on the website within 15 days.
