About the Document

A domicile certificate is an official document issued by Indian state governments to certify an individual as a permanent resident of a specific state or Union Territory. It is primarily used to claim state-level quotas for education and government jobs, as well as to access scholarships and welfare schemes.

Eligibility

  • Individuals living in Maharashtra for a significant period (often 15+ years).
  • If your parents are permanent residents of Maharashtra.
  • Female applicants married to a permanent resident of Maharashtra.
  • Owning property in the state can also support eligibility.

Required Documents

  • School Leaving Certificate / Birth Certificate
  • Aadhar Card
  • Ration Card
  • Light Bill or Property Tax Receipt
  • NOTE: If the age of the applicant is less than 18 years, domicile certificate of the father of the applicant should be attached.

Application Process

  1. Register: Visit the Aaple Sarkar portal and create an account.
  2. Log in: Enter credentials to access the dashboard.
  3. Select Service: Choose 'Revenue Department' and select "Age, Nationality, Domicile Certificate".
  4. Fill Application: Complete the form with personal and address details.
  5. Upload Documents: Scan and upload required documents, including photos and proof of residence.
  6. Pay Fee: Remit the application fee (approx. ₹50).
  7. Submit & Track: Submit the form and use the generated application number to track status.
Fees: approx. ₹50
-While official charges are minimal, total costs might be ₹200-₹300 when including all submission requirements, especially if using third-party services or needing affidavits. Processing Time: 15–30 working days

Where to Apply

Online: Aaple Sarkar Portal
Offline: Nearest Passport Seva Kendra
